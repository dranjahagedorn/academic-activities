About

I am a Leipzig-based researcher and educator working at the intersection of entrepreneurship, AI, and learning design. My work spans academic teaching, applied research, and coaching — always with an eye on how knowledge actually changes practice.

My teaching philosophy is grounded in TZI (Themenzentrierte Interaktion) facilitation principles: balancing individual, group, and subject matter in dynamic, inclusive environments. I have designed and delivered courses across Germany, Vietnam, Indonesia, and Poland — often simultaneously navigating significant cultural and institutional differences.

What excites me most right now is AI as an enabler for founders — how it can support the full startup journey from early planning and validation through MVP development to go-live, as a holistic companion rather than a point solution. This connects my research on coaching and venture creation directly with applied AI tooling.

The goal is not to apply frameworks for their own sake, but to help founders develop their own judgment.

Teaching Philosophy

Experiential and inclusive learning environments grounded in TZI facilitation — balancing individual, group, and subject matter. I consider intercultural humility foundational to effective global education, shaped by teaching across Vietnam, Indonesia, Poland, and Germany. Increasingly complemented by AI-powered tools for active reflection and coaching.
